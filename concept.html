Definition

  • Definition in Dictionary

Medicine, also called pharmaceutical drug or medication, is a chemical substance used to treat, cure, prevent, or diagnose a disease a disease or to promote well-being.

  • Definition in pharmacist law

According to the pharmacist law, a medicine refers to item that:

1.Items that have been embroidered in the Korean Pharmacopoeia and are not medical supplies.
Korea Pharmacopoeia has selected the most popular and important pharmaceutical original medicine, basic preparation and mixed agent, and sets the standard of quality standards in Korea, so all that is written here shall be regarded as medicine.

2.Items that is used for the purpose of diagnosis, treatment, mitigation, treatment or prevention of a person or animal disease, not an instrument, machine, or device.

3.Items that is used for the purpose of having a pharmacological effect on a person's or animal's structural function and that is not an instrument, machine, or device
