Definition

  • Side effect
    Any unintended effects of administering medicine at normal doses.
  • Adverse Event
    Undesirable and unintended signs, symptoms, or diseases that occurred during the administration or use of medicine.
    It is not necessary to have a causal relationship with using medicine.
  • Serious Adverse Event
    Adverse Event refer to items that:
    • Causing death or life-threatening.
    • Need to Hospitalization or extension of hospitalization period.
    • Causing continuous or major malfunction or deterioration.
    • Causing congenital deformity or abnormality.
    • Other Medically Important Situation.


Dose

  • All substances are positions; there is none which is not a poison. Only the dose makes the poison. -Paracelsus

  • Dose-Response Curve

    It indicate relationship between dose and effects, and generally takes the form of a sigmoid curve.
    In most cases, there is no toxicity at low capacity.
    The point at which the toxin is first shown is called a threshold dose level.

  • Therapeutic Index

    It is a figure that confirms medicine safety.
    Therapeutic Index = TD50/ED50
    The smaller number, the safer.


Food interaction

Some foods can help the medicine to its full effect, while others can cause side effects.

  • Grapefruit juice - Blood pressure, Antidepressants, Allergy
    Interrupt liver metabolism, so cause an abnormality in medicinal efficacy.

  • Acid fruit juice - Medicine
    Change the acidity of the stomach.

  • Beer - Depression medicine
    There are side effects caused by certain ingredients.

  • Milk, Dairy product - Antacid, Digestive, Cold
    Disturb the absorption of medicines into the body.

  • Tannin - Antibiotic, Vitamin, Anemia
    Disturb the absorption of medicines into the body.
