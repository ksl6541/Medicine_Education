Basic Principle

Enzyme is involved in proliferation of germs and cancer cells in human body.
Applying a substrate that binds to the active position of the enzyme, such as a padlock and key, and a inhibitor that can competitively bind to the active position can inhibit the action of the enzyme.


As shown above, when the enzyme and the substrate are combined, the product is separated from the enzyme. And this product causes disease.

Therefore, inhibitors should first be combined with enzymes to prevent the substrate from binding with enzymes.
This is basic principle of medicine.


Order

1.Absorption
 The process of a medicine's transition from the administration site to blood and lymphatic fluid.

2.Distribution
 The process of the transfer of medicine in blood to tissues and cells.

3.Metabolism
 The process of a medicine being converted into compound with different chemical structures by enzymes in the body.

4.Excretion
 The process that drugs or their metabolites are released from the body.
